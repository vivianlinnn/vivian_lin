COGS 127: realTalk

Case Study Overview

Teenagers who use social media struggle to represent themselves authentically due to societal pressure to curate a perfect image, leading to self-conscious posting behaviors and reduced confidence in self-expression.

Why should recruiters or hiring managers care about our design project?

Social media is a vital instrument in teenagers’ daily lives that impact their activities, connections, information, and communication. Studies show that teens spend around 3 - 5 hours per day on social platforms which aligns with our user research of analyzing how much time our interviewees spend as well. At the same time research from organizations like the APA, and Pew Research Center focus on the connections between social media use and things such as self-esteem issues, social comparison, and pressure to present an idealized personal image, especially apparent within teenage girls. This is supported by our participants describing situations to retake photos to appear more “aesthetic”, feeling pressure to meet beauty and social standards, and experiencing pressure even from apps designed to avoid these problems.

How is our project addressing a large-scale and significant problem?

Most major platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, or Snapchat, all focus on polished highlight reels. These perfectly curated facades are rewarded with likes, aesthetics, and engagement which can reinforce comparison and validation issues. BeReal, an app made to highlight authenticity and personality, still results in curating certain behaviors rather than true self expression. By allowing retakes you encourage more polished content. To try and eliminate some of the pressure that’s created by these posts, we propose introducing a reflective prompt that mimics journaling before users are asked to take a photo relating to the topic. This way we create an opportunity to showcase healthy self expression and promote less social compassion and appearance based anxiety. With the addition of this, users could make more genuine connections instead of the performative interactions to boost engagement. This would address the problem of social media promoting true self expression while their design encourages judgment and curation. BeReal would become a more accurate picture of authenticity while keeping their “realness” factor.

How can we show that our product would improve a company's metrics or the state of society in a meaningful way?

- Increased user retention: Users are less likely to disengage due to stress or pressure (e.g., missing post windows or feeling judged) - Higher posting frequency: Reducing pressure makes users more willing to post consistently - Stronger user engagement quality: Content becomes more meaningful, increasing deeper interactions rather than passive scrolling - Improved user trust & brand differentiation: Positions BeReal as a platform that actually supports authenticity—not just markets it - Reduced churn in early users: Especially important for users who currently abandon the app due to discomfort or lack of connection

User Research

User 1: Lien, 17

Daily time on Social Media: 3 Hours

Lien is an avid social media user including BeReal. She often struggles with meeting societal beauty standards which led her to take a long-term break from social media when it starts affecting her. Lien appreciates the app’s mission towards a more carefree and authentic experience, but also raised the issue of the two minute timer not giving users enough time to snap their BeReals especially when the app crashes or the user is busy at the moment. Because of preexisting societal judgment from the online community towards teenagers, the timer only spiked their anxiety of posting for fear of being labeled “late” and limits user control/freedom, which is a main heuristic for a successful design according to Nielsen. Lien thinks the current constraints that attempt to keep the authenticity just feel forced and scare her. Therefore, she would like to see a feature that would encourage people to use BeReal the way it was designed, maybe making it more fun or less stressful due to the timer.

User 2: Jyanna, 17

Daily time on social media: 4 hours 40 minutes

Jyanna is another active social media user, but has not used BeReal before. The user research phase of this project marks her first time using the app. Jyanna never struggled with self esteem issues or mental health and is confident with her online presence. She does not feel vulnerable to issues of comparison or societal beauty standards and therefore, has never felt the need to take a break from social media or felt overwhelmed with any content they see of others. She appreciates the minimalistic design although the pictures did not turn out the way she expected, and being able to express her authentic self as the post is only shared with friends. However, she finds the user experience very boring especially because when very few of her friends use the platform and recommended allowing the user to find suggested posts from other users outside of their followings.
